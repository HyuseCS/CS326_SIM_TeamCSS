# Ethical Impact Assessment

## 1. Stakeholders
- **End Users**: Individuals using the application to communicate or interact.
- **Developers (Team CSS)**: The team responsible for creating, maintaining, and updating the software.
- **System Administrators/Moderators**: Personnel managing the platform and ensuring community guidelines are followed.
- **Third-Party Service Providers**: Providers of underlying infrastructure (e.g., hosting, database services).

## 2. Potential Harms
- **Data Breaches**: Unauthorized access to user data, leading to the exposure of personal information.
- **Abuse and Harassment**: The platform could be misused by bad actors to harass, bully, or spam other users.
- **Exclusion/Accessibility Issues**: Users with disabilities might struggle to use the application if it is not designed with accessibility in mind.
- **Misinformation**: Spread of false information within the platform's communication channels.

## 3. Mitigation Strategies
- **Data Security**: Implement strong encryption (e.g., HTTPS, password hashing), secure authentication, and regular security audits to prevent data breaches.
- **Content Moderation**: Provide reporting tools for users, implement automated filtering for inappropriate content, and establish clear community guidelines.
- **Accessibility Standards**: Design the UI/UX following standard accessibility guidelines (e.g., WCAG) to ensure the platform is usable by everyone, including screen-reader compatibility and high-contrast modes.
- **User Empowerment**: Give users control over their privacy settings, including who can contact them and what information is visible.
